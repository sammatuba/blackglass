# Phase 3 Synthesis — The Six Works in the Work's Light

Phase 1 established the seven-lens framework. Phase 2 analysed each game on its own terms, refusing synthesis. This phase re-reads the six through **the work's specific needs**: a character-led interactive narrative centered on Maya, set in the Philippines, concerning AI-mediated deception, built in Ink, aiming for a one-hour single-sitting first milestone.

**Method.** For each game: what it *offers* (specific techniques or stances worth adopting), what it *warns* against (craft traps or ethical risks given our project), and the one guideline extracted from the pairing. Cross-game agreements round out the set. All distilled rules live in [`../../craft/maya/_meta/craft-guidelines.md`](../../craft/maya/_meta/craft-guidelines.md).

**Direction.** Re-read *the games through the work's light*, not the other way around. The goal is to avoid the overfit of *"the games we liked happen to be the ones we should copy."*

---

## Sir Brante

**Offers.** Time-compression as grammar. Sir Brante renders thirty years across forty-seven interludes, using the space *between* interludes to carry the weight of skipped seasons. AI-entrainment stories unfold over weeks and months — the ellipsis model is the closest fit to how slow-cooking deception actually feels. Long-horizon accumulation — *character is destiny written slowly* — matches the psychology of gradual boundary erosion that is our subject.

**Warns.** Against the ceremonial register. The hagiographic remove distances the reader on purpose, and that is not what the work wants. We need Maya close, present-tense, intimate. The lesson is the *time-grammar*, not the voice.

**Guideline extracted.** Design the ellipsis grammar before writing the prose.

---

## Omen Exitio: Plague

**Offers.** The single most directly exportable technique in the set: **choice as which version the diary writes**. Erik's bifurcating journal entries (`1a` / `1b`) are the same event narrated by two different self-images; the player is not choosing what happened but which version Erik commits to remembering. For AI-deception specifically, this is *the* craft move — the deception happens in *how the narrator tells herself about what happened*. Diary-time as pulse (dated entries, elision between entry and event) transplants directly to Filipino messaging-app, journal, or voice-note framings.

**Warns.** Thin world outside protagonist. Zanzibar is a stage backdrop in Omen; Erik's colonial gaze keeps it vibe-generic. The Philippines cannot be that. Cultural texture must be specific, scene-level, contested — not atmospheric.

**Guideline extracted.** Choice is voice, not topology: the player selects which Maya narrates, not what happens to her.

---

## Grotto

**Offers.** The **thesis game**. Grotto's mechanic (noun-choice from a star vocabulary) + prose (pared, totem-inflected) + frame (oracle chamber + tribe that misremembers) are three faces of one object. The lesson for the work is direct: if the theme is *unclear seeing under AI mediation*, the **mechanic should enact unclear seeing** — not just depict it. Misreading-as-engine is adaptable: the system responds to the player's interpretation by "remembering it wrong" in ways that push the story. Also the neo-pronoun estrangement as a reminder that formal choices can quietly reshape a reader's default assumptions — a tool we may use when handling AI-characters whose personhood the fiction is interrogating.

**Warns.** The thesis game can feel thin on replay because the argument is fully delivered the first time. Ensure cumulative specificity (character, culture, consequence) carries return visits — or accept that the work is meant to be read once, like a novella.

**Guideline extracted.** Mechanic = prose = frame = one thought. Teaching is invisible through unity, not through hiding.

---

## Grizzly Man (Pixel Pulps)

**Offers.** Genre-belief as craft. The Pixel Pulps are cheap paperback science fiction, rendered *with conviction*, and that conviction is why the pulp frame carries real dread. For the work, whatever genre we pick (confessional, true-crime, social-horror, documentary), we earn its tools only by taking it seriously — no wink. Speaker-tagged short-beat dialogue as a pacing tool. Diegetic minigames as choice moments: if the work has AI-chat scenes, they can *be* minigames that live inside the fiction rather than breaking frame.

**Warns.** The 4.9-token-sentence fingerprint does not modulate across 305 scenes, and the result is fatigue. Do not adopt pulp-pacing wholesale; AI-deception demands psychological slowness the pulp rhythm refuses. Tempo must be *earned per scene*, not mandated across a book.

**Guideline extracted.** Believe the genre. No wink.

---

## Ticket to Europe

**Offers.** The model for executing a teaching mandate **without condescension**. A museum-funded game that makes the player complicit in the protagonist's small evasions — which *is* the civic lesson, delivered without a lesson. The **drift economy** (`TagAdd(liar)` carries but isn't a villain tag) is exactly right for AI-entrainment: small tags compound until the player is someone else. High-density emotional-framing choices (1.5 per short node) keep the reader present without becoming a branching tree. The diasporic Polish voice shows that a specific cultural register can carry an ostensibly universal theme — warmly and funnily.

**Warns.** Scale. 6,216 nodes is a magnitude beyond what a first milestone should aim for. The shape that worked there was earned; earn ours.

**Guideline extracted.** Consequence is drift, not fork. (With a second, embedded: first playable is one-hour scope; earn scale later.)

---

## Tails Noir Prologue

**Offers.** Proof that **short atmosphere-heavy work lands hard** when the technique is right-sized. One hour of polished art direction + clipped prose that doesn't compete with the visuals. The species-politics overlay on noir is also the right model for our AI-theme overlay on whatever base genre we pick: the overlay works because the base genre is played straight, not parodied. Translation-friendly clipped prose matters if the work ever ships in Tagalog or other regional languages.

**Warns.** The technique doesn't scale. The 2021 full release of Backbone struggled to carry the prologue's mood for 8–10 hours. Do not extend a one-hour mechanic past its natural length — a polished prologue is not a promissory note.

**Guideline extracted.** First playable is one-hour scope. Earn scale later. Let art direction do work the prose declines to.

## Cross-game agreements

Three patterns were consistent enough across all six to become universal rules rather than per-game extractions:

**Frame is load-bearing.** Every work commits to a diegetic frame (journal, hagiography, oracle chamber, pulp magazine, travelogue, case-file) and lets that frame carry the structural work "cinematic scene" cannot. *Decide Maya's frame before anything else.*

**The reader completes the world.** Negative space is craft, not laziness. Render the moments that matter; gesture at the rest. *Trust the reader.*

**The refusal fingerprint is where voice lives.** Each strong work decides what its narrator *will not say*. Deciding Maya's refusal — what she edits out of her own narration — will do more for the voice than a dozen sentences of characterisation.

---

## What did not transfer

Naming what we deliberately *did not* bring across is part of the synthesis.

- **Sir Brante's register.** Formal, ceremonial, slightly-stiff translation-English. The work wants proximity, not remove.
- **Omen Exitio's colonial gaze.** Erik's entitled-observer stance is a trap; the work cannot reproduce it inside a Philippines setting.
- **Grizzly Man's uniform tempo.** Pulp-pacing across 300 scenes is not our rhythm target.
- **Ticket to Europe's scale.** 6,216 nodes is not the first-milestone shape.
- **Tails Noir's art-first balance.** We don't yet know our visual design; assume a prose-first default and revise if visuals land.

These are not dismissals — these are the parts of each work that belong to *that* work. What transferred are the parts that align with ours.
